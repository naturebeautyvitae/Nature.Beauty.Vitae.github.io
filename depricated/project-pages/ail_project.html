Agency and Intentions in Language
Agency and Intentions in Language (AIL) is a series of annual interdisciplinary workshops on topics related to the representation of agency and intentions in natural language. The goal of these workshops is to bring together scholars in linguistics, philosophy, psychology, and other disciplines who are interested in questions connected to agency, intentions, reasoning about actions, and causation, broadly construed.
Description
Our sense of agency and ability to distinguish between intentional and accidental actions are fundamental for social interaction. They allow us to plan and perform joint actions and assign responsibility for our own actions and those of others. Research on the nature of agency and intentions has been very fruitful over the last few decades in philosophy, psychology, and linguistics. However, interdisciplinary discussion has rarely taken place. AIL workshops are designed to provide a platform for such a discussion.
